Your Own Version

Now that you know how to create a Metaphor Maker program, it's time to write your own version! First, you'll need to come up with 3 metaphors. Following our basic metaphor structure, your metaphors should each contain 2 nouns joined by a conjugation of "to be" (am/is/are). In our previous Metaphor Maker, we used "is", but you can use "am" or "are". You can use the same conjugation for all three, or you can mix it up. Write these metaphors on a piece of paper.

Next, come back to the code editor below. On lines 1-6, write out each separate noun in your metaphors. Here's the basic structure of your variables and values:

noun_1a = First noun of first metaphor

noun_2a = First noun of second metaphor

noun_3a = First noun of third metaphor

noun_1b = Second noun of first metaphor

noun_2b = Second noun of second metaphor

noun_3b = Second noun of third metaphor

Now, let's teach your prorgram the metaphor formula. Here's the structure for your code on lines 8-10:

noun_1 = noun_1a + " your to-be verb conjugation " + noun_1b

noun_2 = noun_2a + " your to-be verb conjugation " + noun_2b

noun_3 = noun_3a + " your to-be verb conjugation " + noun_3b

Finally, let's use the print() function to display your metaphors. Write this code on lines 12-14:

print(noun_1)

print(noun_2)

print(noun_3)

Click the Run button to see your metaphors!
