Similes from Literature

Here are a few similes you may have read:

"The horse was strutting like a prizefighter." - Laura Hillenbrand

"There is no frigate like a book / to take us lands away." - Emily Dickinson

"What happens to a dream deferred? / Does it dry up / like a raisin in the sun?" - Langston Hughes

"Character is like a tree and reputation its shadow." - Abraham Lincoln

These may look familiar to you were in the simile generator that you saw on the previous page. But did you notice that in our simile generator, these similies were scrambled? That's what you're going to do with your simile generator: You're going to take real similes and use them in your program to create new similes by scrambling them.
